Gardening Tips

Simple, practical advice to help your garden thrive.

Start with healthy soil: Good soil supports stronger roots and healthier plants. Add compost or organic material to improve structure and fertility.

Water wisely: Water deeply and consistently rather than giving shallow, frequent waterings. Early morning is usually the best time.

Match plants to light: Learn whether your chosen plants need full sun, partial shade, or full shade before planting.

Use mulch: Mulch helps keep moisture in the soil, reduces weeds, and stabilizes temperature.

Prune carefully: Remove dead or crowded growth to improve air circulation and encourage healthier development.

Watch for pests: Check leaves and stems regularly so you can deal with pests before they spread.

Choose the right container: Container plants need enough room for roots to grow properly.

Plan by season: Garden care changes throughout the year, so adjust watering, feeding, and planting based on the season.
